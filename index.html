What is a Carbon Footprint?

Your carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide (CO₂), released into the atmosphere as a result of your everyday actions — from driving a car and charging your phone, to the food you eat and clothes you buy.

Why It Matters

The more CO₂ we emit, the more we contribute to global warming and climate change. Understanding your carbon footprint is the first step to reducing it and creating a more sustainable future.

Tips

Heal the world, make it a better place for you and for me and the entire human race.

Eat More Plant-Based Meals

Reducing meat and dairy can significantly cut your carbon footprint.

Reduce Food Waste

Nearly one-third of all food is wasted — wasting resources and emitting methane.

Choose Greener Transport

Walk, bike, or take public transport to lower transport-related CO₂.

Drive Less, Drive Smart

Eco-driving and trip planning can reduce emissions and fuel use.

Use Energy-Efficient Appliances

Efficient appliances consume less electricity and save money.

Switch Off Unused Devices

Standby power ("vampire energy") wastes electricity.

Buy Less, Choose Well

Sustainable fashion and smart shopping reduce emissions and waste.

Reuse, Recycle, and Donate

Recycling reduces landfill emissions and saves raw materials.
